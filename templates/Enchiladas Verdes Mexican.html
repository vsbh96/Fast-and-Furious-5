Mexican Cuisine Enchiladas Verdes

Mexican Cuisine


Enchiladas Verdes Authentic Recipe is here

Ingredients required:


  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 1 pound (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups (480ml) chicken broth
  • 1 pound (450g) tomatillos, husked and rinsed
  • 1 small white onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 jalapeño peppers, seeded and chopped
  • 1/2 cup (15g) chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup (50g) crumbled queso fresco
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) sour cream
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:


  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a large pot and cover with the chicken broth. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer until the chicken is cooked through, about 20-25 minutes.
  3. Remove the chicken from the pot and let it cool. Shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces with two forks.
  4. While the chicken is cooking, make the tomatillo sauce. Combine the tomatillos, onion, garlic, and jalapeño peppers in a blender or food processor and puree until smooth. Add the cilantro and pulse a few times to combine.
  5. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the tomatillo sauce and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  6. Dip each tortilla in the tomatillo sauce to coat both sides, then place some shredded chicken in the center and roll it up. Place the enchiladas seam side down in a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  7. Pour the remaining tomatillo sauce over the enchiladas and sprinkle the queso fresco on top.
  8. Bake the enchiladas for 20-25 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Serve the enchiladas hot, topped with a dollop of sour cream.
